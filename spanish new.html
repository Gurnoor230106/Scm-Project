Practice Translations

  • The book is on the table. - El libro está en la mesa.
  • I like to eat pizza. - Me gusta comer pizza.
  • What time is it? - ¿Qué hora es?
  • My name is John. - Me llamo John.
  • Where is the bathroom? - ¿Dónde está el baño?
  • She likes to read books in the park. - Le gusta leer libros en el parque.
  • They are going to the store. - Van a la tienda.
  • Can you help me with this? - ¿Puedes ayudarme con esto?
  • My sister is learning Spanish. - Mi hermana está aprendiendo español.
  • I want to visit Spain someday. - Quiero visitar España algún día.
  • How are you feeling today? - ¿Cómo te sientes hoy?

Learning Tips

1. Practice Daily

Consistency is key. Set aside at least 15 minutes every day to practice Spanish.

2. Immerse Yourself

Watch Spanish movies, listen to Spanish music, or change your phone's language settings to Spanish.

3. Label Items Around You

Place sticky notes with Spanish names on items in your house (e.g., "mesa" for table). This builds vocabulary naturally.

4. Find a Language Exchange Partner

Practice speaking with a native Spanish speaker. You can find language exchange partners online or in local meetups.

5. Learn Common Phrases

Focus on learning everyday phrases and expressions to quickly improve your conversational skills.
